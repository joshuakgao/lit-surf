## Definition

Intuitive physics is commonsense understanding of how physical objects and forces behave—the ability to predict how objects move, collide, rest, and interact without explicit calculation. Core concepts include object permanence (objects don't disappear), solidity (objects don't pass through each other), continuity (motion is smooth), and contact dynamics (forces produce motion).

## Origins

Core concept in cognitive development. Extensively studied in human infants (Baillargeon, Spelke et al., 1992+), primates (Cacchione & Krist, 2004), and other animals. "Core knowledge" hypothesis (Spelke, 2000; Carey, 2000) proposes physics understanding is innate or early-developing, not learned from observation.

## Key variants / extensions

- **Object permanence**: Objects continue to exist even when occluded
- **Solidity**: Objects cannot pass through each other; collisions have consequences
- **Continuity**: Objects move along continuous paths, not teleporting
- **Contact physics**: Objects in contact exert forces; objects rest on supports
- **Liquid physics**: Fluids conform to containers; pour, splash, mix
- **Soft-body dynamics**: Deformable objects stretch, compress, tear

**Contrasting views on origin:**
- **Nativism** (core knowledge): Physics is hardwired or innate
- **Empiricism** (learned emergence): Physics understanding emerges from experience and prediction
