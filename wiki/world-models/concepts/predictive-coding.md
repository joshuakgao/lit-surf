## Definition

Predictive coding is a neuroscience and cognitive science hypothesis that the brain learns and functions by maintaining internal models that predict sensory input, and updating these models when predictions are violated (prediction error). The brain is fundamentally a prediction machine trying to minimize surprise.

## Origins

Classical proposal by Rao & Ballard (1999). Developed by Friston (Predictive Processing / Free Energy Principle, 2010+) and Hohwy (2013). Aligns with Helmholtz's "unconscious inference" (1860s) and Bayesian brain hypothesis.

## Key variants / extensions

- **Predictive processing**: Hierarchical predictions at multiple levels; errors propagate up to update higher-level models
- **Free energy principle**: Unifies predictive coding with thermodynamics and information theory; brain minimizes surprisal/free energy
- **Action as inference**: Actions are not separate from prediction; moving reduces prediction error by changing sensory input to match expectations
- **Computational instantiation**: JEPA and video prediction models provide AI realizations of predictive coding principles
