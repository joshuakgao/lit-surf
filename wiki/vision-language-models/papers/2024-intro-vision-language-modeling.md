## Summary

This educational paper provides a comprehensive introduction to Vision-Language Models, explaining what VLMs are, how they work, and how to train them. It addresses the fundamental challenge that while language is discrete, vision operates in a much higher dimensional space where concepts resist easy discretization. The paper surveys VLM architectures and training methodologies, covering contrastive, masking, and generative strategies that form the foundation of modern vision-language systems. Following the success of Large Language Models, VLM research has accelerated with applications ranging from visual assistants to text-to-image generation models.

## Key contributions

- Provides accessible introduction to VLM concepts, architectures, and training approaches
- Explains the dimensional and representational differences between language and vision domains
- Surveys major training paradigms (contrastive, masking, generative) used in VLMs
- Discusses practical considerations for VLM development and deployment

## Method

Educational survey of VLM architectures and training methodologies, covering foundational concepts and practical implementations.

## Results

Comprehensive reference material for understanding VLM landscape and connecting vision-language research to broader deep learning principles.

## Relation to prior work

Synthesizes foundational VLM concepts and serves as accessible entry point to the vision-language modeling literature, building on CLIP and related foundational works.

## Open questions / limitations

As an introductory paper, some advanced topics or very recent developments may not be covered in depth. Field evolves rapidly, potentially limiting long-term comprehensiveness.
