## Definition

A set of 3D spatial points sampled from object surfaces in Euclidean space, each represented by (x, y, z) coordinates in a Cartesian system. Point clouds may include additional attributes such as color (RGB), intensity (from LiDAR reflectance), or semantic labels. Advantages over 2D images: freedom from perspective distortion, true 3D geometric information, direct metric measurements, robust to viewpoint changes.

## Origins

Generated by active sensors (LiDAR) and passive sensors (multi-view stereo photogrammetry, structure-from-motion). Key acquisition platforms: Terrestrial Laser Scanning (TLS), Airborne Laser Scanning (ALS), Mobile Laser Scanning (MLS), UAV platforms, Synthetic-Aperture Radar (SAR). Point clouds became practical for large-scale applications with advances in sensor technology and computational processing.

## Key variants in construction monitoring

**TLS (Terrestrial Laser Scanning)**: High-accuracy, high-resolution point clouds from ground-based scanners; excellent for confined indoor construction spaces; limited by occlusions and scanning geometry.

**ALS (Airborne Laser Scanning)**: Large-scale coverage from airplane platforms; lower density; suited to exterior progress tracking over wide construction sites.

**MLS (Mobile Laser Scanning)**: Continuous scanning from moving vehicles; good for linear infrastructure (roads, bridges) and large horizontal areas; semi-automated data acquisition.

**UAV Photogrammetry**: Structure-from-Motion reconstruction from drone imagery; practical accessibility; lower cost; variable accuracy depending on camera calibration and flight planning.

## Acquisition considerations

- **Point density**: Higher density enables finer detail but increases processing cost; construction monitoring typically uses 1-5 cm spacing
- **Scanning geometry**: Viewing angles affect occlusions and measurement uncertainty; construction sites challenge with multiple viewing directions
- **Temporal resolution**: Frequent scans capture rapid changes but increase labor; typical construction monitoring at weekly/monthly intervals
- **Registration**: Alignment between temporal epochs critical; errors propagate through subsequent analysis
- **Noise and outliers**: Real-world point clouds contain measurement noise, reflectance artifacts, and dynamic objects (people, equipment)

## Practical challenges

- Handling occlusions from construction equipment, temporary structures, and site complexity
- Point cloud registration accuracy in dynamic construction environments
- Classifying temporary vs. permanent objects
- Real-time processing at scale
- Cost-benefit optimization of acquisition frequency vs. monitoring granularity
